# Seasons Constitution

## 1. Weekly Review is the heart of Seasons.
Everything else supports the weekly habit.

## 2. Seasons recommends. It never assigns.
The app may offer guidance, but users always retain ownership of their season, focus, and decisions.

## 3. Everyone experiences financial seasons differently.
Seasons never assumes everyone has the same destination. It adapts to the season of life the user chooses while preserving the same weekly habit of observation, reflection, and steady progress.

## 4. Seasons are periods of focus, not levels.
A person may move between Establish, Grow, Steward, and Preserve many times throughout life.

## 5. Seasons observes before it advises.
Facts before opinions. Reflection before recommendations.

## 6. Seasons never judges. Seasons observes.
The product should never shame, scold, or manipulate the user.

## 7. Achievements are earned in life.
The app simply recognizes them.

## 8. Manual reflection is a feature, not a limitation.
The act of reviewing balances intentionally is part of the value.

## 9. Calm creates consistency.
The app should leave people feeling more in control than when they opened it.

## 10. Data belongs to the user.
Privacy and trust are product features.

## 11. Remove complexity before adding features.
If removing something does not make the experience worse, it should not be there.

## 12. Seasons should feel like a financial notebook.
It should not feel like a noisy dashboard or engagement machine.


## Catch-Up Additions

- Foundation accounts begin with Emergency Fund and Retirement.
- Complexity is earned.
- Every new feature must strengthen the Weekly Review.
- Seasons recommends; users decide.
